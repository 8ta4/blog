Going through a list of thousands of vocab words to decide what to study is a drag. Filtering these words in a spreadsheet or Anki is a pain.

I built this plugin so that cleaning up vocab lists in Neovim is a breeze. You can scroll through a pile of vocab, ditch the words you know, and make a list of what you need to learn.

You can press `s` (see) to open the word under your cursor in multiple Chrome windows at once. This lets you see various reference sources, like dictionary definitions and Google Images, side by side.

Even though the browser opens these windows, the focus stays inside Neovim. You can mark the word as known with `d` (done), flag it for review with `a` (again) or remove it with `x` (x‑out).

This plugin talks to Chrome via a Unix socket behind the scenes. But using this plugin isn't socket science.

The code is [here](https://github.com/8ta4/sift).

I'd love any feedback!
